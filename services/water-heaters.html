Call Pacific when you notice

  • No hot water or water that runs cold too quickly
  • Rust-colored water, popping sounds, or leaking around the tank
  • A pilot light or ignition issue that keeps returning
  • A water heater that is aging out and costing more to run

How Pacific handles it

  1. Inspect the heater, connections, venting, and visible plumbing.
  2. Explain whether repair, flushing, or replacement makes the most sense.
  3. Complete the work cleanly and review maintenance steps before leaving.

Frequently asked questions

Should I repair or replace my water heater?

It depends on age, leak location, performance, and repair cost. Pacific explains both paths so you can choose the practical option.

Do you work on tankless water heaters?

Yes. The page is structured for both tank and tankless service, including diagnostics, flushing, repair, and replacement.

Is no hot water an emergency?

It can be urgent, especially for families or businesses. Call Pacific and the team can help prioritize timing.
